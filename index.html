Services

These days, every business needs a web presence, but most don't have the time, resources, or know-how to make a site on their own. There's where I come in!

I create fast, optimized, custom websites that fit your business needs. Get exactly what you need, whether that's a simple one page site or a complex web application.

Price is set per project, so you'll know the full cost before agreeing to anything - no hidden fees! Depending on your project, you may pay no ongoing fees.

Have questions? Keep scrolling for an overview of the process, and check out the FAQ below.

When you're ready to get started, the first step is to contact me to discuss your project.

How it works

  1. Initial call

    We'll meet and discuss what you're looking for, including, the scope of your project, your timeline, and your budget.

    Typically this takes place over Zoom, but if you're in the Ottawa area I can come to you.

    Contact me to schedule this call.

  2. Proposal

    I'll create a project outline, including a mockup of the proposed site, a clear description of the features, and the price and schedule for the full project.

    You decide if this will work for you - no pressure to commit if it isn't what you're looking for!

  3. Project Contract

    If you're happy with the proposal, we'll both sign a project contract.

    The project contract just puts everything in writing: the details of the site, the timeline, the price, etc. It explains who is responsible for what, and what will happen if something goes wrong, since it's always best to sort that out ahead of time!

  4. Site Creation

    I'll give you regular updates as I create the site. I want to create something you'll love, so you'll have lots of opportunities for feedback.

  5. Site Launch

    When everything is complete and you're happy with the site, I'll launch it!

    From there, the site is all yours! I'll transfer the site code and the hosting to you.

  6. Ongoing Support (Optional)

    If you choose, I offer an option for ongoing support: a regular fee guarantees some of my time set aside for you each month.

    I can update anything on your site, or fix any technical issues you run into.

    This is totally optional, and you can opt out at any point.

FAQ

Why pay for a site when I can use a website builder?

Website builders can work for some people, but there are some real disadvantages: the three big ones are ongoing costs, customisation, and [learning curve].

The biggest disadvantage is price: most website builders have costly monthly fees that can add up quickly. I price per project, so you'll always know how much you're paying up front, and it's a one-time fee.

Most website builders have limited options and have you select a premade style, with limited ways to change it. The websites I make are completely customized to you, so you get exactly what you want - no detail is too small to change.

There's often a steep learning curve: it can take so long to figure out the software that it costs you more in time than you would have paid for a custom site. Adding extra features like scheduling or online store can get even more complicated, and mistakes can have a real financial impact on your business. And of course, something always seems to go wrong when you need to make an update in a hurry! I offer a retainer option that ensures I'll always have time to make a quick update for you.

Do you use templates?

That's up to you! Working from a template makes the process faster and cheaper, but I'm happy to design something completely from scratch if that's what you prefer. Any sites created from templates will have a lot of customization, so even if I start from a template, you can still get exactly what you want.

What if I change my mind halfway through?

Not a problem! Everything will be laid out clearly in the project contract, including what happens if you choose not to complete the project.

What's the difference between a website and a web application? Which one do I need?

This is a simplification, but to give you the basic idea:

A website will always show you the same thing, no matter who is using it. For example, think of a site for a bookstore that shows the bookstore hours and location: no matter who is looking at the site, it's always going to show the store hours and location. It's a lot faster, simpler, and cheaper to create.

Most small businesses only need a website.

A web application changes depending on who is looking at it. Think of social media: when you sign in, you see your personalised feed. When someone else signs in, they see their own personalised feed. The site changes based on who is looking at it. These can take a lot longer to create, can be pretty simple or extremely complex, and generally need ongoing maintenance.

Most businesses who need a web app hire full time software developers to build and maintain the web app - though there are exceptions!
